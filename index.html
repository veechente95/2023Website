About Me

Embracing Vulnerability: My Journey into the World of Tech and Code

I have always been fascinated by the world of tech but was clueless as to where to start and sometimes felt like I was never good enough. However, I learned to accept the vulnerability and excitement to start again, stretch my limits, and teach myself how to code.

My Goal

As a first generation Mexican-American, I acknowledge the lack of diversity in traditional STEM fields and want to break the stigma by providing a diverse open door for future POC and Latinx folks coming from different communities, backgrounds and technical abilities. I want to use my story to help guide others who may have also experienced similar cases of imposter syndrome and help inspire them.

My Experience

Green Jobs Fellow - Los Angeles Cleantech Incubator (Aug - Oct 2023)

Utilized technology in the management of building efficiency measures and energy consumption. Through this program, I aim to grasp the core concepts of building science, systems, and energy efficiency, guided by the Building Performance Institute (BPI).

Data Engineer - Data Science for All by Correlation One (Apr - Jul 2023)

Collaborated with a team to analyze the predictability of the Silicon Valley Bank (SVB) failure using historical stock data from Yahoo Finance API and Alpha Vantage. We built a data pipeline using Python and Amazon S3 while analyzing extensive datasets through Pandas, SQL, and Excel. Presented our findings to an audience of 200+ people.

Data Manager - ACCE / SHLA (Nov 2019 - May 2022)

Developed data visualizations for the Alliance of Californians For Community Empowerment (ACCE) and Stay Housed Los Angeles (SHLA) using Tableau dashboards to effectively communicate tenant needs and inform outreach efforts resulting in an increased response rate and higher tenant satisfaction. Implemented data sharing and management protocols for LA County, Liberty Hill Foundation and 12+ community-based organizations, resulting in improved data accessibility, efficiency, and collaboration.

GIS Researcher - EYCEJ (May - Aug 2019)

Published research through the Marina Pando Social Justice Research Collaborative (MPSJRC) by East Yard Communities For Environmental Justice to investigate the health impacts and environmental injustices on communities in Los Angeles County using GIS terrain analysis to highlight 1,500+ TRI (Toxic Release Inventory) facilities resulting in 20% of all sites located within a 2-mile buffer zone around the 710 freeway.
